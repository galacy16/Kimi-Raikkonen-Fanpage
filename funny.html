Funny Moments

One of Formula 1’s most iconic and famous names bows out of the sport at the end of this season, with Kimi Räikkönen announcing his retirement from the grid earlier this season. Kimi Räikkönen is renowned for not saying much in or out of the car, but behind the wheel, and behind the reporters microphones his plain-spoken (occasionally a little too plain-spoken) radio messages and interviews have become the stuff of legend. The Flying Finn has accrued a cult-like following over the years for his blistering pace and succinct approach to the media, never saying more than he needs to and quite often being more than comfortable to show sheer disdain for the circus that surrounds the pinnacle of motorsport. Ultimately, he's a driver that's purely been in the sport to drive the finest machines on the planet and, with a world title under his belt and several other iconic victories and championship challenges to boot, you'd have to say he did a pretty good job whilst he was in the field. Superb on the track and equally as amusing off it, we run down a few of his most iconic moments in Formula 1 as the curtain begins to fall on the career of the sport's most experienced star.

2006 Brazilian Grand Prix

Martin Brundle asked Räikkönen why he missed a ceremony where Pelé presented Michael Schumacher with an award.
Martin Brundle: “Kimi, you missed the presentation by Pele.”
Kimi Räikkönen: “Yeah.”
MB: “Will you get over it?”
KR: “Yeah. I was having a shit.”

Interview

Another funny interview with the Iceman, where we found out once more that he does not like the media appearances.
Interviewer: “The most exciting moment of race weekend?”
Kimi: “I think so it’s the race start, always.”
Interviewer: “The most boring?”
Kimi: “Now.”

J. Clarkson interview

In the TV show Top Gear, Kimi had a hilarious interview with Clarkson, where, between others, he gave this answer to Clarkson.
Jeremy Clarkson: “The helmet has a special meaning for many drivers. How important is it to you?”
Kimi Räikkönen: “It protects my head.”

Leave me alone

On lap 20 of the 2012 Abu Dhabi Grand Prix, Räikkönen had just taken the lead from Lewis Hamilton and his race engineer Simon Rennie advised that Fernando Alonso was five seconds behind and that he would be kept informed of Alonso’s pace; Räikkönen replied: “
Just leave me alone, I know what to do!”

About Hamilton

Lewis Hamilton said that winning his first race felt better than having sex. A reporter informed Kimi of this. Kimi’s reply to that was:
“Maybe he never had sex.”

Alfa Romeo

The importance ot the helmet for Kimi.
Interviewer: “Do you have any special rituals when the helmet is concerned like many have?”
Kimi: “I wipe it so that I can see better.”
